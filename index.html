Ride this out. It will pass.

Urges peak and fade, usually within 15–20 minutes, even when they feel unbearable right now. You don't have to act on it to make it stop.

Right now, try one of these

  • Delay it. Set a timer for 15 minutes and do nothing about the urge until it ends — you can always come back to it after.
  • Get up and move. A brisk walk or even 20 jumping jacks burns off the restless energy driving the urge.
  • Splash cold water on your face or hold a cold drink — it interrupts the spike and gives your body something else to focus on.
  • Breathe in a box: 4 seconds in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold. Repeat 5 times.
  • Call or text someone — don't explain, just talk. Connection short-circuits the urge faster than willpower does.
  • Physically leave the situation: close the app, log out, put your phone in another room.
  • Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can hear, 3 you can touch — it pulls your brain out of autopilot.

If you place this bet anyway

  • Your current streak of 0 days resets to zero, and you start over from today.
  • The relief is short — most people report the dopamine spike is followed by regret within the hour, not lasting satisfaction.
  • Money spent here is money pulled from whatever it was meant for — bills, savings, or the people who count on you.
  • It gets harder to trust yourself next time, and harder for people close to you to trust you too.
  • One bet rarely stays one bet — it tends to open the door to the next, and the next.
